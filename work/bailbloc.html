Bail Bloc

Bail Bloc is a project that turns unused computer processing power into tangible action against systemic injustice. By mining Monero cryptocurrency, Bail Bloc generates funds to post bail for individuals in ICE detention, allowing them to fight their cases from outside the system of forced detention.

How it works

When you download and run the Bail Bloc app, a small portion of your computer's unused processing power is used to mine Monero, a secure and private cryptocurrency. Every month, the Monero is converted into US dollars and donated to the Immigrant Bail Fund, which uses it to post bond for people in ICE detention. 100% of the funds go directly to those in need.

The Need for Bail Bloc

Immigration bonds in the US are disproportionately high, with the median bond in Connecticut at $15,000, nearly double the national average. Many individuals are forced to remain in detention simply because they cannot afford bond. This project highlights the cruel nature of the cash bail system and works to alleviate its effects.

Impact

Since its launch, Bail Bloc has raised over $5,000 in just two months, helping individuals secure their freedom and fight deportation from outside detention. The project continues to grow, turning passive participation into an impactful abolitionist effort.
